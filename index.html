CUSEC 2015 uOttawa Delegation

What is CUSEC?

The Canadian University Software Engineering Conference is an annual event held in Montreal since 2002. It is a non-profit conference organized by the students for the students. CUSEC brings students from across the country together to learn, network and talk about software engineering and computer science.

The uOttawa Delegation

This year, my goal as Head Delegate is to bring THE LARGEST EVER uOttawa Delegation to CUSEC 2015 at a reasonable price. I also hope to encourage the members of our delegation to participate in CUSEC events and show other schools what uOttawa is made of.

Frequently Asked Questions

What will I get from going to CUSEC?

CUSEC is a gathering of some of the most passionate and brightest minds who come to speak to you about their field of expertise. They talk about things we might never cover in a classroom setting. CUSEC is a place where you learn about the field more than you ever did or ever will in first year university.

CUSEC is also a place where you can show off some of the side projects you've been working on at Democamp. We also have hiring managers and engineers from top companies critique your resumes at Resume Roast.

Finally, the opportunities at CUSEC are endless with our 3-day job fair with some of the world's leading innovative companies. If you are looking for an exciting co-op or internship opportunity, you don't want to miss this chance to speak to these recruiting teams in person.

I was so excited, I already bought my ticket! Can I still get reimbursed?

Yupp! Just send your receipt to ottawa@cusec.net and don't forget to fill out the Google Doc! However, please note that only Faculty of Engineering students are eligible for funding at the moment (please see elibility FAQ).

FUNDING ELIGIBILITY: Am I able to get some money back?

At this time, you are eligible for funding if you are a Faculty of Engineering student in the Winter 2015 term. This covers engineering and computer science students both at the graduate and undergraduate level. Co-op students are also eligible for funding.

I'm eligible for funding, so can you give me a ballpark how much money I am going to get back?

The amount of money you will get back depends on a number of factors: how many uOttawa delegates we end up bringing and the amount of funding that we get. I cannot guarantee the amount of money you will get back but my goal is at least 50% funding for eligible Faculty of Engineering students.

I'm not a software engineer or a computer scientist, should I still go to CUSEC?

100% YES! In fact, we encourage people who aren't in software or CS to come to CUSEC. Prime example: your head delegate is a chem eng, for heavens sake. However, I only have funding for Faculty of Engineering students at the moment. However, if you love to code or are interested in learning more about technology, CUSEC is the perfect crash course for that. You'll have so much fun, you're gonna wanna come over to the dark side ;).
